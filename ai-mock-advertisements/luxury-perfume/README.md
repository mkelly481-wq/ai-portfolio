# Elixir de Privilège — AI Ad Campaign

Concept ad campaign · Gemini TTS · Gemini image gen · VO · Print · Luxury / Satire

gemini-tts · gemini-image · ai-voiceover · ai-image-gen · ad-production · prompt-engineering · luxury · satire

## Overview

A fully AI-produced concept advertising campaign for *Elixir de Privilège*, a fictional ultra-luxury fragrance brand. The campaign leans into campy, self-aware satire of high-end perfume advertising — playing the tropes completely straight while winking at the audience. Tagline: *Smell Like Your Dad Owns the Bank.*

Scripts were creatively directed and refined by me, with AI assistance — the voice, tone, and comedic framing were driven by detailed creative briefs that established character, class persona, and comedic register before generation began.

## Deliverables

**Voiceover spots — Gemini TTS**

- **:30s** — hero spot; Mid-Atlantic accent, vault SFX, full product introduction with comedic CTA
- **:15s** — social cutdown; private jet ambiance, punchy flex energy, "it's not just a fragrance; it's a tax bracket"
- **:06s** — bumper/pre-roll; instant ego, marble table thud, "smells like you've never seen a price tag"

**Print advertisement — Gemini image gen (Google AI Studio)**

- **Magazine horizontal full-page** — ornate crystal perfume bottle on black marble, Monaco yacht harbor at golden hour through open doors; title and tagline overlaid in gold

## Tools & approach

- **TTS engine:** Google Gemini TTS
- **Image gen:** Google Gemini (via Google AI Studio)
- **Voice:** Single narrator — Mid-Atlantic accent, smooth and deeply smug
- **Prompting:** Detailed character and tone briefs established before generation; SFX direction and pacing notes embedded in scripts; TTS production notes included for voice tuning (speed, pitch, phonetic pronunciation)
- **Scripts:** Creatively directed and human-refined; AI-assisted

## What to notice

The comedic register here required more precise creative direction than a straight ad — the line between effective satire and just being weird is thin, and holding that tone across three spot lengths takes intentional scripting. Each cutdown escalates the smugness while tightening the message. The :06s in particular strips everything down to a single absurd image: "smells like you've never seen a price tag." The print ad matches the tone visually — photorealistic, aspirational, and just slightly too much.
